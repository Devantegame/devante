# Devante: The Spellbook Card Game — Rulebook v6

*For 2–6 players. This edition incorporates all playtested amendments and supersedes the original printed booklet.*

## 1. Objective

Collect the most of the eight Devante cards. The game ends when the COLLECT pile runs out; whoever holds the most Devante cards wins. Ties are broken by the highest-ranking Devante held (Corium, ranked 1st, beats all).

## 2. Game Contents

- 14 Character Cards (including 3 Assistant Cards: Getergrin, Smookers, Rupert)
- 8 Devante Cards (ranked: 1st Corium, 2nd Aurum, 3rd Lignum, 4th Metallum, 5th Rubrum, 6th Caeruleum, 7th Aurantiaco, 8th Rosea)
- 44 Ability Cards (20 Spell, 8 Crystal Ball, 8 Broomstick, 8 Potion)
- 30 Object Cards (**10** Forgotten Goblet, **10** Blank Parchment, **10** Dusty Candelabra) *(amended from 13 each)*
- 2 Dragon Cards

## 3. Setup

1. Shuffle all cards together.
2. Turn over the top 3 cards to form the face-up DISCARD pile. Any Devante card revealed this way is shuffled back into the deck and replaced.
3. Deal 8 cards to each player. Devante cards dealt this way are yours — to hold, to play, or to lose to thieves.
4. The remaining deck is the face-down COLLECT pile. Every Devante not dealt is in there, waiting to be drawn.

## 4. Your Turn

Draw one card from the COLLECT pile (**if your hand is empty, draw two on that turn**), then make **one** play:

1. **Discard** one card to the DISCARD pile. *(Amended: discarding a Character card does nothing — it grants no extra cards. The old "discard characters to draw" rule is removed.)*
2. **Attack** — play a Character card with matching Ability cards (and optionally one Assistant) against other players.
3. **Set a shield** — play Odorf with Potion cards, placed face-up in front of you.
4. **Play a Devante card** in any of its four ability modes.

### Collecting when you play *(new rule)*

When you make a play with Character / Assistant / Ability cards **against another player**, you draw cards from the COLLECT pile equal to the total number of cards you played (character + assistant + abilities). You do **not** collect when merely discarding cards.

### Assistant Cards (Getergrin, Smookers, Rupert)
Cannot be played alone. Pair one Assistant with a Character + Ability cards to double the effect (quadruple with an Advanced Character). Only one Assistant per play.

### Spell Cards
Play Spell cards with a spell-trait Character to steal cards from opponents. One steal per spell (two if the Character is Advanced; doubled again with an Assistant).

**Multi-target spells** *(new rule)*: when playing multiple spells at once, you choose how to divide them between players. Example: a Character with 5 Spell cards could take 2 cards from Player 1 and 3 from Player 3. You then collect cards equal to everything you played (here, 6: the character + 5 spells).

- The target may shuffle their hand before cards are taken.
- Defenders may counter with Broomsticks (see below) or a Devante played as a Broomstick.
- After your spell attack resolves, your turn ends.

### Crystal Ball Cards
Play with **Diamone** to view opponents' hands.

**Multi-target scrying** *(new rule)*: multiple Crystal Ball cards may be played together, one target per ball. Diamone + 3 Crystal Balls lets you view the hands of three players of your choosing — and you collect 4 cards (3 for the balls, 1 for Diamone).

- A protected target's Potion cards absorb your crystal units first; you need one spare unit remaining to view that hand.

### Broomstick Cards
Defensive. When targeted by Spells or Crystal Balls, counter by discarding a broomstick-trait Character plus one Broomstick per incoming ability card (you cannot play more Broomsticks than the cards attacking you). Each Broomstick cancels one incoming unit. **Every card used in the defence — the Character included — is refunded from the COLLECT pile.** Example: a rival attacks with a Character + 3 Spells (they collect 4 back); you counter with a Character + 2 Broomsticks — only 1 card is stolen, and you collect 3 back. Defending does not use up your turn. Broomsticks cannot stop Devante cards.

### Potion Cards
Pair with **Odorf** and place face-up in front of you as a standing shield. Each attack unit against you removes one Potion to the DISCARD pile; when the last Potion goes, Odorf goes to the DISCARD pile with them. Once placed, you cannot add more Potions in later turns.

### Object Cards
No ability — discard dross and spell-theft chaff.

### The Dragon Card (×2)
Play a Dragon to the DISCARD pile (it targets no one) and immediately draw 4 cards from the COLLECT pile.

### Devante Cards
You gain Devante cards three ways: dealt in your opening hand, drawn from the COLLECT pile, or stolen from opponents. Holding them is how you win — but each can also be played at any time during your turn, without a Character, wielding immense power:

- **As a Spell:** steal **8 cards**, divided between any players however you wish.
- **As a Crystal Ball:** view **every player's hand**.
- **As a Broomstick:** repel **any number** of spells or crystal balls in one block; draw two cards from the COLLECT pile.
- **As a Potion:** place in front of you as a standing shield that absorbs **4 attacks** before burning.
- **As a Shield-Breaker:** shatter **all** of one player's potion shields (or their Devante shield) in a single blow. It permits no further action — the Devante then goes to the BURN pile.

Broomstick cards are powerless against a Devante; only a higher-ranking Devante can block one.

Rules: only a **higher-ranking** Devante can block a lower one (Corium ranks highest). One Devante per player per turn, whether attacking or blocking. Every Devante used in play is revealed and moved face-up to the BURN pile — so all players can count how many remain in circulation.

## 6. End of Game & Tie-breaking

The game ends the moment the COLLECT pile empties. Most Devante cards wins. Ties are broken by the highest-ranking Devante held — effectively the lowest rank number, as 1st Corium beats all.

## 7. Pile Rules *(amended)*

The DISCARD pile is **never** reshuffled into the COLLECT pile. (The former "standard" reshuffle rule is removed; what was the Quick Gameplay Variation is now the only way to play.)
